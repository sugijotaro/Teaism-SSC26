### What problem is your app playground trying to solve and what inspired you to solve it? 200 words or less.

Watching my younger siblings struggle daily with entrance exams and relentless assignments, I felt a deep urge to help them find a moment of peace. While I have personally practiced Zen for years, I found its abstract nature difficult to share with them effectively.

My perspective shifted through my experience as a Starbucks barista. I realized that whether it is a coffee or a bowl of matcha, a single cup can serve as a vessel for mindfulness and human connection. I saw that the essence of the Japanese tea ceremony—"Teaism"—is not about rigid rules, but about finding beauty in the ordinary act of drinking.

This App Playground solves the "accessibility of mindfulness." It bridges the gap between ancient Zen philosophy and modern life. By transforming the simple, daily act of drinking into a ritualized "Teaism" experience, I can share the tranquility of Zen with my siblings and others overwhelmed by a high-stress society. Furthermore, it aims to provide a spiritual anchor to those caught in the superficial "Matcha boom," reintroducing them to the true heart of the tea ceremony. It is a digital invitation to find stillness in a single sip.

### ​​Who would benefit from your app playground and how? 200 words or less.

This app benefits anyone struggling to find stillness in a high-stress society, from students like my siblings to busy professionals. It offers two distinct pathways to peace: Solo Mode and Pair Mode.

In Solo Mode, users transform a universal daily habit—sipping a drink—into a meditative ritual. While starting a new mindfulness practice can feel daunting, everyone drinks something throughout the day. By following intuitive, step-by-step guidance based on "Teaism," users learn to handle each cup with intentionality, turning a brief break into a moment of profound mental clarity.

For those who wish to "gift" or share mindfulness, Pair Mode creates a unique space for connection. By placing an iPad between them, family or friends can engage in a structured ritual that facilitates honest dialogue. The power of Teaism helps lower social barriers, allowing users to express feelings—like gratitude or deep-seated thoughts—that might otherwise feel too awkward to say out loud.

Crucially, the app is beverage-agnostic. Whether it is coffee, tea, or water, the experience remains the same: reclaiming one’s time and nurturing relationships. Users emerge not just refreshed, but more connected to themselves and those they cherish.

### How did accessibility factor into your design process? 200 words or less.

Just as the tea ceremony is an "art of the five senses," I designed this app to integrate sight, sound, and touch, making the spiritual essence of "Teaism" accessible to everyone, regardless of their location or physical ability.

Living in Japan, I have easy access to tea rooms, but many—especially those abroad—do not. To bridge this gap, I included cinematic visuals and authentic environmental sounds. These audio cues provide a sense of presence for visually impaired users while deepening immersion for all. My voice narration guides each step, allowing for hands-free interaction—vital for users with vision challenges, including my own struggle with cataracts.

I emphasized tactile engagement by requiring a physical beverage; as the app progresses, users experience real-world warmth and aroma. To ensure physical inclusivity, I simplified the interface to minimal taps and swipes, removing the barrier of formal postures like seiza (kneeling). Furthermore, tiered commentary provides intellectual accessibility for all knowledge levels. This holistic approach ensures that anyone, regardless of their physical or cultural background, can experience the profound tranquility of the tea ceremony through a sensory-rich, inclusive interface.

#### Describe which AI tools you used, what you used them for, and what you learned. 200 words or less.

I utilized Gemini, GitHub Codex, and Adobe Photoshop AI to enhance both the conceptual depth and the technical implementation of my project.

As a two-time Swift Student Challenge winner (2020 and 2025), I led the overall application architecture and logic. I used Codex as a high-velocity assistant to scale the app’s various modes and extensive content. This allowed me to focus on the complex experience design while using the AI to accelerate the coding of specific tasks based on my established implementation policies.

For cultural localization, I leveraged Gemini to simulate a "global persona." This was vital for translating the unique, often untranslatable nuances of Zen and tea culture into accessible English "Teaism." These dialogues helped me identify potential cultural barriers, ensuring that someone with zero prior knowledge of Japanese traditions could still find meaning in the experience. Photoshop AI was used to refine my original photography for visual consistency.

Through this process, I learned that AI is most effective as a tool for empathy. By challenging my own cultural biases through simulated global perspectives, I gained a deeper understanding of how to communicate complex spiritual concepts inclusively, ultimately creating a more universal user experience.

### What other technologies did you use in your app playground, and why did you choose them? 200 words or less.

I chose SwiftUI because its declarative nature perfectly mirrors the minimalist philosophy of Zen. It allowed me to build a clean, distraction-free interface that keeps the user’s focus entirely on the ritual.

To create a truly "Five Senses" experience, AVFoundation was indispensable. I used it to orchestrate the playback of the high-definition cinematic videos and environmental sounds recorded at the museum. By carefully managing audio-visual layers, I was able to transform the iPad into a portable tea room. These technologies were chosen not just for their functionality, but for their ability to turn an abstract spiritual concept into a concrete, immersive, and sensory-rich reality.
